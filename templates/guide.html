Your Guide to Smart Nutrition

Understanding calories and how your body uses them is the first step toward achieving your health goals.

What is a Calorie?

A calorie is a unit of energy. Specifically, it's the amount of energy needed to raise the temperature of 1 kilogram of water by 1 degree Celsius.

When we talk about food calories, we're actually talking about kilocalories (kcal). One food calorie equals 1,000 regular calories.

Understanding TDEE

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day. It's made up of several components:

Components of TDEE:

  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate):

    The calories your body burns at rest to maintain basic life functions.

  • NEAT (Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis):

    Calories burned through daily activities like walking, fidgeting, and standing.

  • Exercise Activity:

    Calories burned through intentional exercise and sports.

  • TEF (Thermic Effect of Food):

    Calories burned during the digestion, absorption, and processing of food.

The Power of NEAT

NEAT can vary significantly between individuals and can make a huge difference in your daily calorie burn. Here are some common NEAT activities:

Daily Movement

  • • Walking to work
  • • Taking the stairs
  • • Shopping
  • • Household chores

Workplace Activity

  • • Standing desk usage
  • • Walking meetings
  • • Fidgeting
  • • Taking breaks to move

The most common formula used is the Harris-Benedict equation: BMR = 655 + (9.6 × weight in kg) + (1.8 × height in cm) - (4.7 × age in years) for women, with a different constant for men.
